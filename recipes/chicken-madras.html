Chicken Madras

Description

A madras curry is a hot curry with a dark red thick sauce (the colour comes from the chillies and tomatoes). It's got slightly tangy notes to it from the addition of tamarind and fenugreek. The chicken does not have to be marinated in this dish, however we will be. The sauce is usually a smooth sauce. We're using a madras curry paste in this recipe, but will also add in a few extra spices. To get the lovely smooth sauce, we'll be using passata (rather than tinned tomatoes - which are quite lumpy). Best served with naan and/or rice. Four portions.

Ingredients:

  • Butter
  • Oil
  • Chicken breast or thigh (750g)
  • 2 red onions
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 1 sachet of "Geeta's Madras Curry Paste"
  • Tomoto puree
  • Passata (500g)
  • Water (200ml)
  • Fresh corriander- Optional
  • Seasonings- you do not need to use the exact same as we do, however we will say what we have used in the steps below. Use your favourite, or whatever you have available!

Steps:

  1. Cut your chicken breast into small bite-sized chunks and marinate with your choice of seasonings. (We used: Salt, pepper, paprika, oregano, garam masala, garlic powder, onion powder & tumeric.)
  2. Heat your pan to a medium-high heat and pour your oil in.
  3. While you wait for your pan to heat up, chop your onions and garlic and place in a bowl/plate near you- ready for later.
  4. When you pan and oil is hot enough, put in your seasoned chicken. Do not stir or turn your chicken too frequently to aquire a crisper outside to your chicken.
  5. Take out the chicken just before it is completely cooked. Place the chicken on the side while you complete the next steps
  6. Add more oil to your pan if needed, then add your chopped onions.
  7. After 5-6 minutes add your chopped garlic. Let cook for another 3-4 minutes.
  8. Add as much butter and tomoto paste as you would like and stir.
  9. After 2-3 minutes add your "Geeta's Madras Curry Paste" with 200ml of water. Let cook for another 5 minutes.
  10. Add your passata as well as your nearly cooked chicken.
  11. Stir and place the lid on your pot. Leave to simmer for 10 minutes
  12. Enjoy your beautufil Chicken Madras!
